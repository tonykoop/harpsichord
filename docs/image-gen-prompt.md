# Image Generation Prompt

Concept visualization only; not fabrication authority.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 studio product photograph of a finished harpsichord concept with the lid open, emphasizing the plucked action: long wooden case, keyboard, visible strings, bridges, register slides for selectable 8-foot and 4-foot choirs, rows of vertical jacks with tiny plectra, dampers, and a buff or lute-stop rail near the strings. Use warm hardwood casework, pale soundboard, dark key fronts, brass or steel strings, and serviceable action access rather than ornate fantasy decoration. Clean studio background, soft directional studio lighting, realistic room-scale cues, about a 50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic luthier-shop scene of a small harpsichord action mockup and partial instrument on a bench, with a player pressing a key so a jack lifts toward a string. Natural light, spare plectrum material, register slide coupon, and buff-stop test strip nearby, the setting focused on regulation, pluck, reset, and service access.

## Detail / Macro Prompt

Macro photorealistic close-up of a harpsichord jack and tongue assembly: plectrum just beneath a string, damper felt above, guide rail and register slot visible, tiny pivot detail, polished wood jack, brass string, shallow depth of field and precise mechanical alignment.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, no watermarks, no piano hammers, no impossible jack spacing, no fake historical maker name, no extra keyboards unless visibly supported by the case.
