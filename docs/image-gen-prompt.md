# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 studio product photograph of a finished harpsichord concept presented as a single real object with the lid raised on its stick, emphasizing the keyboard-controlled plucked-string action that makes a harpsichord recognizable: a long horizontal wooden case tapering toward the tail, one keyboard of natural and accidental keys at the front, a pale spruce-toned soundboard, slender wooden bridges, taut metal strings running the length of the case, rows of slim vertical jacks rising through the gap behind the keys, and a register slide and a buff/lute stop rail near the strings for selectable choirs. Render warm figured-hardwood casework with a hand-rubbed finish, dark key fronts, brass and steel strings, and clean serviceable action access rather than ornate gilded fantasy decoration. Realistic furniture-scale cues, clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic luthier-shop scene of a small harpsichord action-and-register bench mockup beside a partly-cased instrument, a maker pressing a single key so one jack lifts toward a string while a plectrum coupon, a register-slide coupon, and a buff-stop test strip rest on the bench nearby. Soft natural window light, sawdust and hand tools in the background, shallow depth of field, the framing telling a story of regulation, pluck, quiet reset, and service access — concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro photorealistic close-up of a single harpsichord jack riding in its guide: a polished wooden jack body, a pivoting tongue carrying a thin plectrum tucked just beneath a taut metal string, a tuft of damper felt above, and the register slot framing the jack. Raking side light to catch the wood grain and the bright string, crisp mechanical alignment, fine surface texture, very shallow depth of field, high detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no piano hammers or struck action; no impossible jack spacing or extra keyboards unless visibly supported by the case; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
